# Data Quality Storytelling

Use this structure when presenting findings:

1. **What was analyzed** (dataset + time window)
2. **What was corrected** (key quality issues and why they mattered)
3. **What the evidence shows** (2–3 quantified findings)
4. **What action to take next** (specific and measurable)
5. **What remains uncertain** (limitations and missing data)

## Language rules
- Lead with business impact, not code details.
- Include base-n with every percentage.
- Use “suggests” or “is associated with,” not causal claims.
- Call out exclusions (nulls/sentinels) when they affect interpretation.
